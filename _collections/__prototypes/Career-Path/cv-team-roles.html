Imaging & Diagnostics (7 roles)

Advanced Cardiac Sonographer (ACS)

Provides advanced sonography on an interdisciplinary team through education and clinical experience.

Pathway: Advanced cardiac sonography program or extensive ultrasound experience.

Cardiac Sonographer (RCS or RDCS)

Uses echocardiography to collect and analyze cardiac images for patient care.

Pathway: Cardiac ultrasound clinical experience with or without formal health science education.

Cardiographic Technician (CCT)

Works in EKG, Holter monitoring, and stress testing.

Pathway: Cardiovascular technologist experience or allied health training.

Rhythm Analysis Technician (CRAT)

Analyzes cardiac rhythms and implanted device data using specialized monitoring equipment.

Vascular Specialist (RVS or RVT)

Performs non-invasive vascular testing and assists in vascular disease diagnosis.

Advanced Practice (5 roles)

Nurse Practitioner / Advanced Practice Nurse

Provides advanced nursing care in cardiovascular settings — inpatient, outpatient, and procedural.

Degree: Master’s or doctoral nursing program.

Physician Associate (PA)

Practices medicine under physician supervision across cardiology care settings.

Degree: Accredited PA program (master’s level).

Clinical Nurse Specialist (CNS)

Clinical expert in evidence-based nursing — direct care plus teaching, research, and systems improvement.

Degree: Master’s or doctoral; inpatient, outpatient, and educational settings.

Clinical Pharmacist

Optimizes medication use in collaboration with physicians, nurses, and patients — often in hospital or critical care.

Degree: Doctor of Pharmacy (PharmD).

Therapy & Rehabilitation (4 roles)

Exercise Physiologist

Designs and supervises exercise programs for cardiac rehabilitation and prevention.

Physical Therapist

Helps patients recover mobility and function after cardiac events and procedures.

Occupational Therapist

Supports patients in regaining daily living skills during cardiovascular recovery.

Perfusionist

Operates heart-lung machines and supports patients during cardiac surgery.

Support & Specialized Care (6 roles)

Registered Nurse

Delivers direct patient care across cardiac units, clinics, and procedural settings.

Registered Dietitian

Provides nutrition counseling for prevention and management of cardiovascular disease.

Clinical Psychologist

Helps patients and families manage the mental strain of cardiovascular illness.

Degree: Doctoral degree with supervised clinical practice.

Clinical Social Worker

Coordinates care, navigates insurance, and connects patients to financial and community resources.

Genetic Counselor

Assesses inherited cardiovascular risk and guides patients through genetic testing decisions.

Informatics Specialist

Applies data and technology to improve cardiovascular care delivery and outcomes.
