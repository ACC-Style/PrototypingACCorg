## **Why does the ACC care about well-being?** 

As the professional home for cardiovascular (CV) clinicians, the American College of Cardiology (ACC) is deeply committed to understanding and supporting the health, resilience and well-being of the global CV workforce. Clinician well-being (CWB) is essential to sustaining a strong workforce, ensuring clinicians feel their practice environments are safe and inclusive. Well-being is critical to ensuring high-quality patient care outcomes. 

The College has long advocated for an inclusive and respectful cardiovascular workforce as a foundational strategy to reduce burnout, enhance clinician safety, improve patient outcomes and support long-term workforce sustainability. 

Recognizing the growing impact of burnout across the profession, CWB is embedded in the ACC’s 2019–2023 and 2024–2028 Strategic Plans. The College tracks enterprise-level metrics, including: 

- The prevalence of burnout among members 

- Members’ perceptions of the ACC as a resource for reducing burnout 

The ACC’s Clinician Well-Being strategy was developed to advance understanding of the prevalence, scope and impact of clinician burnout on both the workforce and patient care. This work focuses on: 

- Identifying key drivers and barriers to addressing burnout 

- Understanding factors that enable clinician well-being 

- Cataloging and developing tools to assess and mitigate burnout 

- Evaluating evidence-based, actionable interventions at the individual, team and institutional levels 

## **Visitors to ACC’s Clinician Well-Being page on ACC.org should be able to:** 

1. Understand why clinician well-being is a priority for ACC 

2. Understand the spectrum of clinician well-being, including how burnout affects recruitment, retention and workforce sustainability 

3. Learn about the prevalence, drivers and scope of burnout within the cardiovascular workforce 

4. Explore ACC-developed activities, resources and workforce-related surveys designed to address burnout 

5. Access ACC’s Cardio Safe resources (planned for late 2026), including: 

   - Four 15-minute virtual educational modules 

   - Guidance for institutions and individuals to reduce patient- and visitorinitiated mistreatment 

   - Best practices for reporting and tracking patient and visitor mistreatment 

6. Engage with member stories and personal journeys related to burnout, recovery and resilience
