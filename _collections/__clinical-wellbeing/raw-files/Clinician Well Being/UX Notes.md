## Meeting Summary: Clinician Well-Being Page Refactor

The discussion focused on moving the current Clinician Well-Being page out of its older “Feature” placement and turning it into a more intentional, story-driven suite of pages that better reflects ACC’s commitment to supporting clinician well-being. The current page has valuable content, but it mostly functions as a collection of links. The team agreed there is an opportunity to make the experience more compelling, useful, and scalable.

The biggest shift is from **“here are some links”** to **“ACC understands the pressures clinicians face and has built resources, initiatives, education, advocacy, and member-driven support to help.”**

The page should not just preserve existing content. It should clarify the story, elevate the value, organize resources around clinician needs, and create a structure that can grow over the next year or two.

---

## Core Project Goals

### 1. Move from a link list to a story-driven experience

The current page has useful material, but it does not clearly tell the user why the work matters, why ACC is investing in it, or how the resources connect. The refactor should give users a stronger sense of purpose and direction.

### 2. Help clinicians quickly find the right support

The redesigned experience should organize content around user needs, such as burnout, patient mistreatment, workload, resilience, advocacy, education, and system-level stressors. The goal is to make the page scannable and self-guiding.

### 3. Show that clinician well-being is a serious ACC priority

The page should communicate that well-being is not a side topic or a temporary COVID-era concern. It connects to ACC’s mission, member value, advocacy, education, quality improvement, and the long-term health of the cardiovascular workforce.

### 4. Preserve and refresh valuable existing content

Some COVID-era resources may still be useful if scrubbed, reframed, or updated. The team discussed keeping what still has value, retiring what is obsolete, and reusing evergreen material where appropriate.

### 5. Create a scalable structure for future initiatives

The team wants a structure that can support new webinars, articles, education modules, resource hubs, grant-funded work, and future work group priorities without feeling duct-taped onto the page later.

### 6. Humanize the section

The design should likely use more people-focused imagery and less abstract brand texture. This is a chance to make the topic feel personal, human, and connected to real clinicians.

### 7. Relocate the page into a more findable IA location

The current thinking is to move the page from the old `/Features/` location into a Tools / Practice Support / Support Resources area, with redirects handled as part of the site move.

## Draft Elevator Pitch

**ACC’s Clinician Well-Being pages help cardiovascular professionals feel seen, supported, and equipped to address the pressures of modern practice. Burnout is not just an individual issue, and it is not solved by self-care alone. Through member-driven resources, education, advocacy, quality improvement, and focused initiatives, ACC brings together practical tools and trusted guidance to help clinicians navigate burnout, mistreatment, workload burden, and system-level challenges so they can sustain their careers and continue delivering excellent patient care.**

A shorter version:

**Clinician well-being is essential to the future of cardiovascular care. ACC brings together resources, education, advocacy, and member-driven initiatives to help clinicians address burnout, navigate workplace pressures, and feel supported across their careers.**

Even shorter, for a hero/subtitle:

**You are not alone. ACC is committed to helping cardiovascular clinicians address burnout, reduce system pressures, and sustain meaningful careers in patient care.**

## Story the Page Should Tell

The clearest message from the meeting was:

**Burnout is real. It is widespread in cardiology. It can come from patients, colleagues, workload, data burden, and systems of care. It is not just about yoga or individual resilience. ACC can help by giving clinicians resources, education, advocacy, and a platform to address the issue from multiple angles.**

That story can be framed around three questions:

### What should users get from this page?

Clinicians should leave with a sense that ACC understands their reality and has practical resources, programs, and initiatives that can help them address well-being challenges.

### Why should users care?

Because the pressures clinicians face are not isolated or personal failures. They are part of larger professional, institutional, and system-level issues that require shared support, education, and action.

### Why does ACC care?

Because healthier clinicians are essential to better cardiovascular care. Supporting clinician well-being connects directly to ACC’s mission to transform cardiovascular care and improve heart health.

## Key Decisions / Directional Agreements
